 SECTION SPECIFICATIONS

 01 — BUSINESS IDENTITY
- Full legal name + DBAs
- Jurisdiction, entity type (Pvt Ltd, LLC, LLC, PLC, sole prop)
- Year founded + trigger
- Registered address + website URL
- Registry IDs: CIN/GSTIN (India, via ZaubaCorps/Tofler) | CH (UK) | EIN (US)
- Parent/subsidiaries/brands
- Operational status
- Mark each: Confirmed official records | Found on website | Unverified

 02 — BUSINESS MODEL
- Core problem + buyer persona
- Product/service delivery mechanism
- ALL revenue streams with mechanics
- Deal size / contract structure / transaction value
- Key resources: tech, people, IP, infrastructure
- Key activities + partnerships
- Cost structure + drivers
- Value chain position
- Scalability: linear or non-linear?
- Extract exact pricing tiers if publicly visible

 03 — REVENUE & FINANCIALS
- Revenue by year: CONFIRMED (cite source) or ESTIMATED (state basis)
- Funding rounds: amount, date, lead investor, valuation
- Pricing: exact tiers found publicly
- Revenue type: transactional / subscription / project / retainer / commission
- Revenue concentration risk
- Financial signals: growth, burn, profitability
- Registry data: MCA (India) | Companies House (UK) | SEC (US)
- If no data: ESTIMATED range with methodology (e.g., team size × revenue-per-employee benchmark)

 04 — CEO/FOUNDER PROFILE
- Full name, approx. age
- Education: institutions, degrees, years
- Complete pre-founding career: employer + role + tenure (chronological)
- Founding trigger: specific catalyst
- Current role: hands-on vs. delegated
- Public presence: conferences, podcasts, articles, key quotes
- LinkedIn URL + follower count + posting themes
- Personality signal: technical / sales / visionary / operational
- Network quality: board, investors, advisors
- Controversies, failures, lessons
- Co-founders: same depth for each

 05 — CLIENT ACQUISITION STORY
- FIRST paying client: how acquired? Warm network / cold outreach / content / luck?
- Initial go-to-market: did hypothesis work?
- Evolution: Phase 1 → Phase 2 → current
- Current channels (SEO, outbound, events, referrals, ads): evidence required
- Sales cycle estimate
- Pricing evolution
- Growth spikes or partnership moments
- Marketing visible: blog, SEO, social, YouTube, podcast
- Source from: founder interviews, website About/Origin, founder posts

 06 — CLIENT ANALYSIS
- Named clients: case studies, press releases, LinkedIn
- Typical profile: company size, industry, geography, buyer job title
- Total client count: CONFIRMED or ESTIMATED (label clearly)
- Concentration risk: top 3–5 clients as % of revenue
- Retention signals: repeat business, long-term contracts, testimonial duration
- Public client feedback: extract specific quotes
- Complaints, churn, negative reviews
- Geographic concentration
- Search: "{company name} case study" + "testimonial" + "reviews G2"

 07 — STRONG POINTS
List 5–10 advantages (tech moat, team expertise, network effects, switching costs, brand/trust, regulatory, geography, data, distribution, IP). For each:
- Name precisely
- Competitive value
- Durability: easy / hard / nearly impossible to copy
- Evidence

 08 — WEAK POINTS RED FLAGS
List by priority: CRITICAL / HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW. For each:
- Name precisely
- Supporting evidence
- Management awareness + response
- Cover: regulatory compliance, financial fragility, single points of failure (person/client/channel), IP vulnerabilities, reputational risk
- Use 🔴 for CRITICAL, ⚠️ for HIGH

 09 — MARKET POSITION
- TAM estimate + methodology
- Estimated market share
- Positioning: leader / challenger / niche / follower
- Top 3–5 competitors: size, product, pricing, geography
- Indirect competitors + substitutes
- Market growth trend + evidence
- Regulatory environment
- Barriers to entry
- Right to win vs. vulnerable areas
- India: also check Tracxn sector, Inc42, SEBI intermediary lists

 10 — KEY PEOPLE
- CTO/Head of Tech: name, background, credibility
- Head of Sales/CRO: name, track record, network
- Key technical leads visible on LinkedIn
- Board: names, background, value beyond name
- Investors/advisors: names + value
- For small companies: 2nd/3rd person roles
- Talent gaps: missing key roles?
- Search: "{company name} team" + LinkedIn employees tab + job postings

 11 — INTELLIGENCE SUMMARY
Paragraph 1: Overall verdict—health, trajectory, quality (evidence-based)  
Paragraph 2: Top 3 non-obvious insights (things casual observers miss)  
Paragraph 3: 7 specific questions requiring primary research (phone/direct data)—each unanswerable from public sources

 RED FLAG CHECKLIST (manual review)

 🔴 CRITICAL (immediate action)
- Regulatory non-compliance (operating without required FCA/SEBI/RBI authorisation)
- Revenue claims contradicted by official filings
- Director disqualification or struck-off status
- Criminal litigation, fraud, regulatory enforcement
- 80%+ revenue from single client, zero diversification

 ⚠️ HIGH (30 days)
- No IP protection on core product
- All revenue from one geography
- Founder is sole technical/sales resource
- No DPDP / GDPR / CCPA compliance
- Revenue growing, cash flow negative, no funding path
- Culture/leadership problems visible in reviews

 🟡 MEDIUM (monitor)
- Website claims unsupported by third-party evidence
- Pricing too low vs. peers — margin sustainability risk
- No moat — easily replicable
- Founder zero public thought leadership
- Dependent on single acquisition channel
